Important! The duration of an assessment digital testing varies according to age, history of previous assessments, the severity of the speech/language deficit, as well as the collaboration and attention of the person.

During the initial parent or client interview, client/parent, physician, and teacher concerns will be discussed. Additionally, we will examine medical history, developmental milestones, language acquisition (bilingual clients), educational history, and family history.

During the informal assessment portion, use a variety of toys, books, or videos to elicit a spontaneous speech and language sample. Then transcribe and analyze the sample to determine if there are any speech and/or language anomalies.

A digital assessment can be conducted for speech and language using digital tools.

Digital Tools

Peabody Picture Vocabulary Test (PPTV)

Description
Peabody Picture Vocabulary Test (PPTV) is a standardized assessment tool used to evaluate receptive vocabulary skills. The patient is simply presented with pictures along with a verbal label presented by the SLP.
Target Area

Receptive Vocabulary Skills

Duration
Approx. 15-20 minutes
Procedure
The patient is presented with 4 pictures. The task is to measure their understanding of vocabulary. With Lipmic, the patient can point out the pictures using their articulation of labels.

Comprehensive Assessment of Spoken Language (CASL)

Description
CASL assessment or the Comprehensive Assessment of Spoken Language is a standardized language assessment and a valuable tool for evaluating several areas of a patient's expressive language skills.
Target Area

Lexical/Semantics, Syntactic Skills

Duration
Approx. 15-20 minutes
Procedure
The patient is presented with various questions. The task is to measure their understanding of vocabulary. With Lipmic, it captures the patient's articulation of their answers.
